# Day 08 – Agile and Scrum

## What is Agile?

**Agile** is a software development methodology that focuses on delivering high-quality software through **continuous development, collaboration, customer feedback, and iterative improvements**. It breaks a project into small, manageable iterations called **Sprints**, allowing teams to adapt quickly to changing requirements.

Unlike traditional models such as Waterfall, Agile welcomes changes even during the later stages of development.

---

# Agile Manifesto

The Agile Manifesto is based on **4 Core Values**:

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools.
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation.
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation.
4. **Responding to change** over following a fixed plan.

---

# 12 Principles of Agile

- Satisfy customers through early and continuous delivery.
- Welcome changing requirements.
- Deliver working software frequently.
- Business and development teams work together daily.
- Build projects around motivated individuals.
- Face-to-face communication is the most effective.
- Working software is the primary measure of progress.
- Maintain a sustainable development pace.
- Focus on technical excellence and good design.
- Simplicity is essential.
- Self-organizing teams produce the best results.
- Regularly reflect and improve processes.

---

# Why Agile?

Agile is widely used because it provides:

- Faster software delivery.
- Better product quality.
- Continuous customer feedback.
- Improved collaboration.
- Easy adaptation to changing requirements.
- Reduced project risks.
- Increased customer satisfaction.

# What is Scrum?

**Scrum** is the most popular Agile framework used to manage software development projects. It divides the project into short development cycles called **Sprints**, usually lasting **2–4 weeks**.

Each sprint delivers a working increment of the software.

---

# Scrum Framework

```text
Product Backlog
       ↓
Sprint Planning
       ↓
Sprint Backlog
       ↓
Sprint (2–4 Weeks)
       ↓
Daily Scrum
       ↓
Development
       ↓
Testing
       ↓
Sprint Review
       ↓
Sprint Retrospective
       ↓
Next Sprint
```

---

# Scrum Roles

## Product Owner

The Product Owner represents the customer and is responsible for:

- Defining product requirements.
- Managing the Product Backlog.
- Prioritizing features.
- Maximizing product value.

---

## Scrum Master

The Scrum Master ensures the Scrum process is followed by:

- Removing team obstacles.
- Conducting Scrum meetings.
- Guiding the Scrum team.
- Ensuring Agile best practices.

---

## Development Team

The Development Team is responsible for:

- Designing the application.
- Writing code.
- Testing the application.
- Delivering working software.

A Scrum Team usually consists of **5–9 members**.

---

# Scrum Artifacts

## Product Backlog

A prioritized list of all project requirements maintained by the Product Owner.

**Examples**

- Login Feature
- Dashboard
- Employee Management
- Leave Module

---

## Sprint Backlog

A subset of Product Backlog items selected for the current sprint.

---

## Increment

A working version of the software delivered at the end of every sprint.

---

# Scrum Events

## Sprint

A fixed development cycle lasting **2–4 weeks**.

Objective:
Deliver a working software increment.

---

## Sprint Planning

The team decides:

- What will be developed?
- How it will be developed?

---

## Daily Scrum (Daily Stand-up)

A **15-minute daily meeting** where every team member answers three questions:

1. What did I complete yesterday?
2. What will I work on today?
3. Is there any blocker?

---

## Sprint Review

Held at the end of the sprint to demonstrate completed work to stakeholders and collect feedback.

---

## Sprint Retrospective

The Scrum team discusses:

- What went well?
- What didn't go well?
- What improvements can be made in the next sprint?

---

# Agile Testing

Agile Testing is a continuous testing approach where testing starts from the beginning of the project and continues throughout every sprint.

Testing is performed alongside development rather than waiting until development is complete.

---

# Role of a Tester in Agile

A QA Engineer is involved in every sprint and participates in:

- Requirement analysis.
- Sprint Planning.
- Test Scenario preparation.
- Test Case writing.
- Test Execution.
- Defect Reporting.
- Regression Testing.
- Sprint Review.
- Sprint Retrospective.

---

# Agile vs Waterfall

| Agile | Waterfall |
|--------|-----------|
| Iterative model | Sequential model |
| Continuous customer feedback | Feedback after completion |
| Changes are welcomed | Changes are difficult |
| Frequent releases | Single release |
| Testing throughout development | Testing after development |
| Faster delivery | Slower delivery |

---

# Advantages of Agile

- Faster delivery.
- Improved customer satisfaction.
- Better collaboration.
- Early defect detection.
- Continuous testing.
- Better product quality.
- Flexibility to changing requirements.
- Reduced project risk.

---

# Disadvantages of Agile

- Difficult to estimate project cost.
- Requires active customer involvement.
- Frequent requirement changes.
- Less documentation.
- Requires experienced team members.

---

# Real-Time Example

Suppose an HR Management System has the following modules:

- Login
- Dashboard
- Employee Management
- Leave
- Recruitment

Instead of delivering the complete application at once, Agile delivers it in multiple sprints.

### Sprint 1

- Login
- Dashboard

### Sprint 2

- Employee Management

### Sprint 3

- Leave Module

### Sprint 4

- Recruitment Module

Each sprint is tested independently before moving to the next sprint.

---

# Best Practices

- Deliver working software frequently.
- Maintain regular communication.
- Encourage customer feedback.
- Keep sprint goals realistic.
- Perform continuous testing.
- Conduct retrospective meetings.
- Improve after every sprint.

---

# Key Learnings

- Agile is an iterative software development methodology focused on continuous delivery and customer collaboration.
- Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework.
- Scrum consists of defined roles, artifacts, and events.
- Testing is performed throughout the sprint rather than at the end.
- QA Engineers actively participate in every stage of the Agile process.
